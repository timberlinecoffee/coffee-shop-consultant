## Claim Classification

This claim characterizes the source of Groundwork's benchmark data used in the financial model and benchmarking feature. Per FTC standards, a "factual assertion" about data sources requires substantiation before the claim is made in advertising.

---

## Data Sources

The Groundwork benchmark figures are derived from the following publicly available and operator-reported sources, as documented in `docs/research/2026-05-15-market-research.md`:

| Source | Type | Data Covered |
|---|---|---|
| Toast POS Industry Reports (2024–2025) | Industry aggregate | Revenue, labor costs, COGS benchmarks for independent cafes |
| Crimson Cup Startup Cost Guide (2025) | Practitioner guide | Startup cost ranges; $150K–$250K for 800–1,200 sq ft |
| SBA / BLS failure-rate data | Government aggregate | Survival rates by year for food & beverage businesses |
| Square Community / Coffee Forums (r/CoffeeShops, r/smallbusiness) | Operator-reported | Operating cost patterns, staffing ratios, pain points |
| Perfect Daily Grind operator interviews | Practitioner interviews | Location, lease, and margin observations from operators |
| Barista Life operator research | Industry publication | Staffing and cost benchmarks from working operators |
| Keys to the Shop operator reports | Practitioner survey | Time-savings and operational benchmarks from members |
| Texas Coffee School research (cited) | Trade education | Business-skills gap data for first-time operators |

**Sample size:** Not a direct primary survey. Data synthesized from secondary industry sources and operator community reports. Estimated representation: hundreds to thousands of operator-reported data points across sources.

**Date range:** 2024–2025 (most recent available at time of research, May 2026).

**Methodology:** Aggregation and synthesis of publicly available industry benchmarks, trade publications, and operator community data. No proprietary primary survey was conducted.

---

## Claim Qualification Rationale

Because benchmarks are synthesized from secondary sources (not a controlled primary survey of a defined operator cohort), the marketing claim must qualify the source. The current approved copy reads:

> "Benchmarks modeled from coffee-shop industry research and operator-reported data"

This is accurate to the methodology and does not overstate the sourcing. The original claim "built from real coffee-shop operators" implied a direct primary data collection that was not conducted and has been corrected per this substantiation review.
