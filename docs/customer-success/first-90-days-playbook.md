# First-90-Days Success Playbook

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Status:** Draft — pending IRB review
**Owner:** Customer Success Manager

> Persona context (from [TIM-523 research memo](../research/2026-05-15-market-research.md)): The #1 cause of coffee shop failure is undercapitalization and cash flow blindness. Owner pain points in order: (1) cash flow, (2) rising operating costs, (3) staffing, (4) no business skills. Timberline's job in 90 days is to become the business partner they did not know they could afford.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Days 1–7)

**Goal:** The owner can see their real gross margin for the first time.

### Milestones

- Shop profile complete
- Last 30 days of expenses entered by category (COGS, labor, overhead)
- First financial snapshot generated
- Weekly 15-minute number review scheduled (calendar block, not intention)

### Day 7 Checkpoint

Owner can answer the question "what is my gross margin right now?" without guessing. This is the baseline. Everything else builds on it.

### Churn Risk Signals

| Signal | Threshold | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Profile not complete | Day 3 | Medium |
| No expenses entered | Day 5 | High |
| No login after signup day | Day 3 | High |

### CSM Intervention

Personal email from CSM. Not a template. Reference their shop name if known. Offer a 20-minute setup call framed as: "Most owners who do this call get unstuck immediately — it is usually one small thing."

---

## Phase 2: Clarity (Days 8–30)

**Goal:** The owner understands their cost structure and can name the biggest drains.

### Milestones

- Category tracking active: COGS, labor, and overhead separated
- First monthly report reviewed with CSM or independently
- Top 3 margin drains identified by name (not "costs are high" — specific line items)
- At least one "what if" scenario run (pricing or cost adjustment)

### Day 30 Checkpoint

Owner knows their real cost structure and can name one specific thing to fix. They are not looking for a feeling — they have a number to act on.

> Benchmark to share: Operating costs in a healthy independent cafe run 75–85% of monthly revenue. Labor should be 25–35%. If either number is out of range, that is the conversation.

### Churn Risk Signals

| Signal | Threshold | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Fewer than 3 logins in two weeks | Day 14 | Medium |
| No data added after initial snapshot | Day 10 | High |
| Support ticket submitted but unresolved | Same day | High |

### CSM Intervention

Direct outreach within 24 hours of any high signal. Goal is to understand the barrier, not to demonstrate features. If no data has been entered: offer white-glove setup — CSM walks through the first month together on a call and enters the data with the owner.

---

## Phase 3: Action (Days 31–60)

**Goal:** The owner makes or plans one concrete business decision using Timberline data.

### Milestones

- Pricing check run on top 5 menu items against cost of goods
- Labor cost trend reviewed week-over-week
- At least one pricing or sourcing change initiated based on what the data showed

### Day 60 Checkpoint

Owner has taken one data-driven action and can explain why they took it. "I raised the 12-oz oat latte by $0.50 because my COGS went up and the margin was at 18%" is the target. Vague wins do not count.

### Churn Risk Signals

| Signal | Threshold | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| No new data entered | 3+ weeks | High |
| Usage drops to login-only, no reports run | Day 45 | High |
| Negative or no reply to Day 14 email | Day 17 | Medium |

### CSM Intervention

Check-in call — not automated, not templated. Share a specific insight from another owner in a similar situation (anonymized). The question to ask: "What would make Timberline useful enough that you would miss it if it was gone?"

---

## Phase 4: Habit (Days 61–90)

**Goal:** Weekly number review is automatic. The owner would miss it if it was gone.

### Milestones

- 4 or more consecutive weeks of active use
- Seasonal pattern recognition begun (even informal — "I know March is slower")
- At least one referral conversation initiated or testimonial shared
- NPS score of 8 or above, or an active conversation about what is missing

### Day 90 Checkpoint

Owner would recommend Timberline to another shop owner without being asked. This is the real activation signal. Retention data supports it: owners who refer within 90 days churn at less than half the rate of those who do not.

### Churn Risk Signals

| Signal | Threshold | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Usage drops after Week 8 | Day 56 | Medium |
| NPS score below 7 | Any time | Critical |
| No engagement with Day 30 email | Day 33 | Medium |

### CSM Intervention

Cancellation-risk call. No script. Listen first, document second. If the root cause is a product gap: route to Chief of Staff as a named product ask with the owner's words. If the root cause is habit: offer a 30-day structured accountability check-in — short weekly calls with a specific review agenda.
