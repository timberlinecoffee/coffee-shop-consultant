# NPS and Feedback Loop Design

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Status:** Draft — pending IRB review
**Owner:** Customer Success Manager

---

## When to Ask

Do not ask for feedback before the owner has used the product. A Day 0 NPS is noise.

| Trigger | Timing | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| First in-product prompt | After first snapshot generated | Caught at the moment of first value |
| Day 14 email NPS | Day 14 | Enough time to form a real opinion, early enough to course-correct |
| Day 90 milestone NPS | Day 90 | Tracks activation and surfaces promoter candidates |

The first-snapshot trigger is the most important. That is the moment the owner sees their gross margin for the first time. If they feel something — good or bad — capture it now.

---

## What to Ask

Keep it to two questions per ask. Owners are busy. A 90-second ask gets answered. A survey does not.

### Day 14

1. "How likely are you to recommend Timberline to another coffee shop owner?" (0 to 10)
2. "What is the one thing that would make this more useful for you right now?"

### Day 90

1. "Has Timberline changed how you think about your numbers?" (Yes / Somewhat / Not really)
2. "What is one thing we got right, and one thing we should fix?"

Do not add a third question. Do not add a comment box to the scale question. Do not add a "tell us more" follow-up unless triggered by a low score.

## How to Close the Loop

Every piece of feedback gets a human close. No feedback goes into a void.

### Score 9–10 (Promoter)

1. Personal thank-you email within 24 hours. Specific, not templated. Reference what they said.
2. Ask if they would be willing to share their experience (testimonial or referral) — one ask, not a campaign.
3. Invite to beta features or an advisory conversation if applicable.

### Score 7–8 (Passive)

1. Thank them for the honest feedback.
2. Share one specific roadmap item that addresses what they said was missing.
3. Check in again at the next milestone. Do not let passives drift to detractors.

### Score 0–6 (Detractor)

1. Personal call within 24 hours. CSM only. No automated follow-up.
2. No script. Goal is to listen and understand root cause, not to retain.
3. Document the verbatim complaint in the Voice of the Customer log.
4. If the cause is systemic (same complaint from 3+ owners): route to Chief of Staff as a named product ask.
5. If one-off: resolve directly and follow up within one week to confirm it is resolved.

---

## Voice of the Customer Log

Maintain a shared log (Notion or equivalent) with:

- Owner name (or anonymized ID)
- Date of feedback
- Score
- Verbatim quote (or close paraphrase)
- Root cause category (product gap / onboarding / pricing / expectation mismatch / other)
- Resolution status and date

Review this log monthly. If any root cause category appears in 3 or more entries in a 30-day window, escalate to Chief of Staff as a priority product ask.

---

## What Not to Do

- Do not send an NPS and then never follow up.
- Do not aggregate scores without reading the text.
- Do not thank detractors without actually calling them.
- Do not ask for a testimonial before Day 60.
- Do not run NPS surveys more than twice in the first 90 days (Day 14 and Day 90 are sufficient).
