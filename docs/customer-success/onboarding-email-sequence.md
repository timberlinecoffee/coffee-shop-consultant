# Onboarding Email Sequence

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Status:** Draft — pending IRB review
**Owner:** Customer Success Manager

> Voice mandate: warm, expert, unpretentious. No em dashes. No "leverage," "unlock," "embark." Read each email aloud before sending. If it sounds like a press release, rewrite it.

> Persona context (from [TIM-523 research memo](../research/2026-05-15-market-research.md)): New owners enter with passion for coffee and exit because of cash flow crises and business skills gaps. They have been sold tools that did not work. They are already paying $37–$2,500 for guidance. They know what good looks like. Show, do not tell.

---

## Email 1 — Day 0: Welcome

**Subject:** You are in. Here is what happens next.

You made a decision a lot of shop owners wish they had made sooner. Most people who open a coffee shop know coffee. The hard part is everything else: the numbers, the pricing, the decisions that compound quietly until they become a crisis.

Timberline is built to help with that. Start with your shop profile and you will have your first real financial snapshot in under 20 minutes.

**CTA:** Set up your shop profile

---

## Email 2 — Day 1: First Step

**Subject:** The one thing worth doing today

The owners who get the most from Timberline do one thing in their first week: they run their first financial snapshot. Most do not know what their gross margin actually is before they do. You need that number before anything else makes sense.

Most people finish in under 20 minutes. No accounting background required.

**CTA:** Run your first snapshot

---

## Email 3 — Day 7: Check-In

**Subject:** One week in. How is it going?

You have had a week with Timberline. We want to know what is clicking and what is not. If you got stuck, tell us and we will sort it out. If you have not started yet, no judgment. A lot of owners get here and realize the numbers feel scarier than the coffee.

Reply to this email or book a 15-minute call. We read every reply.

**CTA:** Book a 15-minute call

---

## Email 4 — Day 14: Progress

**Subject:** Two weeks in. What you should be seeing by now.

By this point most owners have their cost data in and are starting to see their margins clearly for the first time. Operating costs typically run 75 to 85 percent of revenue in an independent cafe. That number surprises most people when they see it laid out.

If you are not there yet, something is getting in the way. Log in, check your snapshot, and reply if anything looks off.

**CTA:** Check your progress

---

## Email 5 — Day 30: Milestone

**Subject:** One month. Did anything change?

A month ago you signed up. We want to know if anything shifted. Even something small counts: a cost you had not tracked before, a pricing call you made with confidence instead of gut feel, a number that finally made sense.

If something still is not working, now is the right time to say so. We would rather know than not.

**CTA:** Share your experience (2 minutes)
