Week 5 — Bar Layout & Equipment  ·  Worksheet

How to use this: Print this out and work through it with a pen, or fill it in on screen. The sketch area is grid-ruled so you can draw your bar to rough scale. You don't need precise measurements — you need an honest picture of what you're working with and where things will go.

Section 3

The Customer Experience Walk

Before the live call, walk through your space — or your sketched space — as if you are a customer coming in for the first time. Go through all five steps below. If you don't have a physical space yet, work through this based on your target layout. Be honest about what you find.

1
Enter

You walk in. Where does your eye go first? Is it immediately obvious where to queue or order?

2
Browse & Queue

You join the queue. Can you read the menu from here? What do you pass on the way to the till?

Retail & Pastry Placement Check

Your pastry display and any retail products (coffee bags, merchandise, gift cards) should sit along the customer's path before they reach the POS — not beside it or after it. A customer who passes the display on the way to the till has already considered adding something. A customer who only sees it after they've paid has already mentally closed their wallet.

3
Order & Pay

You reach the till. Does the staff member feel dedicated to taking your order — or are they also making drinks and juggling things behind them?

4
Wait

You've paid. Where do you go? Is there somewhere natural to stand that's out of the queue?

5
Collect & Leave (or Stay)

Your drink is ready. Can you collect it without walking back through the queue? Does the handoff feel smooth?

Flow Direction Check — the Arrow Test

Go back to your bar sketch from Section 2. Draw two sets of arrows: one showing the path a customer takes from entry to handoff, and one showing the path a barista takes to make your most common drink. Then answer these questions.

Section 5

Equipment Placement Check

Tick each one as you confirm it on your sketch. If you can't tick it, make a note of what needs to change.

Espresso machine is where barista faces the customer or handoff point

Barista can make eye contact and call names without turning around.

Grinder is within one arm's reach of the group head

No stepping or reaching across the body to access ground coffee.

Milk fridge is within one step (or directly below) the steam wand

Barista does not need to turn around or cross the bar to access milk.

Cups are stored within the production zone, within reach of drink build start

No walking to the end of the bar to retrieve cups at the start of each drink.

Knock box is at the barista's working position

Not across the bar, not at the far end — directly where the barista stands.

Each barista has at least 900mm of clear working counter space

Two people working simultaneously won't constantly reach into each other's space.

The till / POS is accessible without crossing the production zone

The person taking orders doesn't need to step through the barista's workflow to process a payment.

Drain and power points confirmed before finalising bar position

Moving a bar because the drain can't reach it is expensive. Confirm this before anything is built.
