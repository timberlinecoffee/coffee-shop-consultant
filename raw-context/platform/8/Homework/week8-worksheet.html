Planning Worksheet

Work through this before assembling your Business Readiness Document. Use it to surface gaps, sharpen your thinking, and arrive at the live call with something real to say.

Week 8 of 8 — Planning Worksheet
Section 1

Your Operational Readiness

Answer honestly. These questions reveal gaps more reliably than any checklist.

Have a plan before you need it — not while you are in the middle of it.
Section 2

Your BRD Consistency Check

Before you assemble, check that the main components speak to each other.

Read your Week 1 Concept Brief and your Week 4 Menu side by side. Does the menu feel like a direct expression of the concept? If not, which one needs updating?
Section 3

Your Three-Year Vision

This is Component 10 of your Business Readiness Document. Write it here first. Not a growth plan — a personal direction statement. Write it for yourself, not for an investor.

In three years, what does a typical week look like for you as the owner?
Are you on the bar every day? Managing from a distance? Running two locations? Be specific.
What does the business look like in three years — size, team, revenue, reputation?
What does this business give you that you could not get another way?
Not the business case — the personal answer.
What would you need to decide or do in the first 90 days to make that three-year version more likely?
Section 4

Your Biggest Uncertainties

These are the most valuable things to bring to the live call — not the things you have figured out.

Section 5

After the Programme — Your Next Steps

Map out your next concrete steps before the live call so you are not starting from scratch the week after the programme ends.

For the Live Call

Each person shares one thing they are most confident about in their Business Readiness Document, and one thing they are still most uncertain about. Prepare both answers honestly before you arrive. The uncertain thing is more valuable — that is where the best live call conversations start.
