Week 8 of 8 — Homework Brief

The video this week covered the operational rhythms that keep a café healthy — daily sales reviews, weekly COGS and labour checks, monthly P&L comparisons, and how to spot problems before they become crises. It also covered the 90-day post-opening adjustment period, growth decisions, and what the longer arc of a successful coffee business actually looks like.

This week's homework is different from every other week. There is only one task — but it is the most important thing you will produce in this entire programme. You are compiling your Business Readiness Document: the assembled, complete version of everything you have built across all eight weeks.

This is not a summary exercise. You are not writing new things — you are assembling what already exists, checking it is complete and consistent, and presenting it as a single coherent document. The ten components listed below are the ones you have been building since Week 1. Most of the work is already done.

Your One Task

This is your capstone. Complete it before the live call and bring it with you — you will share a piece of it with the group.

1
Capstone

Compile your Business Readiness Document

Your Business Readiness Document is the assembled version of everything you have built during the programme. It is not a final essay — it is a practical planning document you will actually use. Each component comes from a previous week's homework. Bring them together into one document, in order, and check that each section is complete, consistent with the others, and reflects what you now know.

Component 1 — Concept Brief (Week 1)

Your shop type, target customer, positioning, and competitive advantage. Before assembling, check it still reflects what you have decided across the other seven weeks — things often shift.

Component 2 — Financial Model (Week 2)

Your 12-month financial model with revenue projections, startup costs, and monthly P&L. Make sure your assumptions still match your final concept, menu pricing, and staffing plan.

Component 3 — Site & Market Analysis (Week 3)

The case for your location and the key lease terms you are targeting or have secured. If your site has changed since Week 3, update this section.

Component 4 — Coffee Program & Roaster Research (Week 4)

Your roaster selection, sourcing approach, and the coffee program at the centre of your concept. This should connect directly to your Week 1 positioning.

Component 5 — Full Menu with Pricing & Margins (Week 4)

Your complete menu with cost per item, sale price, and gross margin for each. Confirm that your menu pricing is consistent with your financial model from Week 2.

Component 6 — Bar Layout & Equipment Plan (Week 5)

Your bar layout, three-zone structure, equipment list, and estimated equipment costs. Check that the layout supports the service speed your financial model assumes.

Component 7 — Staffing & Training Plan (Week 6)

Your job descriptions, two-week training outline, and culture document. Confirm that your staffing costs match your Week 2 financial model.

Component 8 — Pre-Opening Marketing Plan (Week 7)

Your 30-day marketing calendar, opening story in all three formats, and your five community relationships. This should now reflect what you actually did or committed to, not just what you planned.

Component 9 — 90-Day Operations Checklist

Your week-by-week operational priorities for the first 90 days after opening. Produced this week using the 90-Day Operations Tracker template.

Component 10 — Your Three-Year Vision

A single page on what success looks like in three years — not a growth plan, but an honest statement of what you want this business to give you and become. Written for yourself, not an investor.

The assembly check: Read the completed document from start to finish. Does the concept in Component 1 still match the menu in Component 5? Do the staffing costs in Component 7 match the financials in Component 2? Does the bar layout in Component 6 support the service model your concept requires? Inconsistencies are not failures — they are the most useful thing to bring to the live call.

What to Bring to the Live Call

Bring your full Business Readiness Document — or as much of it as you have assembled. The live call is a capstone celebration and a share-out, not a final exam. Each person will share one thing they are most confident about in their document, and one thing they are still most uncertain about. Come prepared to do both honestly. This is also the call where we talk about next steps — what happens after the programme, the in-person offer at Timberline, and what ongoing support looks like. Come with your questions about what comes next.
