Week 1 — Lay the Foundation

Days 1–7  ·  Set up, claim, and announce to people who already know you

Days
1–2
Digital Setup

Claim or refresh Google Business Profile. Set up or update Instagram. Link social bio to email sign-up. Make sure all profile details are complete.

Your Actions
Google claimed
Instagram live
Email sign-up ready
Days
3–4
Personal Network

Tell people who already know you — friends, family, former colleagues, anyone in your personal network. A direct message beats a broadcast post every time.

Your Actions
Personal messages sent
Personal network post done
Days
5–7
First Community Contact

Visit two of your five community partners in person. Introduce yourself. Leave a flyer or card. First contact only — no ask required.

Your Actions
Partner 1 contacted
Partner 2 contacted

Week 1 goal: By end of Day 7 you should have digital presence live, your personal network informed, and two community introductions made.

Week 2 — Tell Your Story

Days 8–14  ·  Go public with your opening story and pitch local press

Days
8–9
Opening Story Post

Post your opening announcement on Instagram. Use your Format B caption from the homework. Make it feel like a real person talking, not a launch announcement.

Your Actions
Opening post live
Shared to Stories
Days
10–11
Press Outreach

Send your press pitch to local food journalists, neighbourhood blogs, and community newsletters. Use your Format A pitch. Give them 2–3 weeks lead time from now.

Contacts Pitched
Pitches sent
Follow-up scheduled
Days
12–14
Behind-the-Scenes Content

Post 2–3 times this week: fit-out progress, your first espresso pull, a team introduction, or your bar taking shape. Show the process, not just the destination.

Content Plan
3 partners contacted
2+ posts done

Week 2 goal: Your opening story is public. Local press has your pitch. Three of five community relationships have been made.

Week 3 — Build Anticipation

Days 15–21  ·  Soft opening, real feedback, and final community outreach

Days
15–17
Soft Opening (if running one)

Invite-only event for 20–40 people. Family, friends, neighbouring businesses, and any press who responded. Be explicit: this is practice, feedback is welcome, service will be slower than normal.

Soft Opening Notes
Invites sent
Feedback collected
Days
18–19
Final Community Contacts

Complete your five community relationships. Follow up with anyone who expressed interest. Confirm any specific arrangements — a flyer on their counter, a cross-post, a mention to their people.

Your Actions
All 5 partners contacted
Arrangements confirmed
Days
20–21
Opening Date Announcement

Post your confirmed opening date publicly. Not a vague "coming soon" — a specific date. Give people something to put in their diary and tell their friends about.

Your Actions
Date announced publicly
Google hours updated

Week 3 goal: Soft opening complete. All five community relationships made. Opening date public. People have a specific reason to show up.

Week 4 — Open Strong

Days 22–30  ·  Team briefing, final logistics, and opening day

Days
22–25
Team Opening Brief

Brief your team on the opening day plan. Culture first, then tasks. Over-staff it. Decide in advance how you will handle a longer-than-expected queue — and brief your team on how to communicate it warmly.

Your Actions
Team briefed
Rota confirmed
Days
26–28
Final Countdown Content

Post 2–3 times in the final week. Build excitement without overselling. Show the team, the space, the product. Remind people of the date and the address.

Content Plan
Countdown posts done
Address + date in bio
Day
30
Opening Day

Be on the floor. Meet people. Collect contact details from every person who comes in. Ask for honest feedback directly. Run a short debrief with the team after service — while it is fresh.

Opening Day Checklist
Team over-staffed for day one
Email/contact collection ready
Feedback plan in place
Post-service debrief scheduled

Week 4 goal: Open on the date you announced, with a team who knows the plan, and a way to capture the details of every person who walks through the door.
