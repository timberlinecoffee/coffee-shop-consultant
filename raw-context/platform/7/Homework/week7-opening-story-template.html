Start Here — Answer These Three Questions First
Your Core Story

Do not write for an audience yet. Just answer honestly. Your three formats below come from these answers.

Why are you opening a coffee shop?
Why here — this location, this neighbourhood?
Why now — what has made this the right moment for you?
What is the one thing you most want someone to feel when they walk in for the first time?

Before you write any of the three formats below: re-read your answers above. The best version of your opening story is almost always the honest one — not the polished one. The press pitch needs structure, but the feeling behind it should come from these answers.

Format A
The Press Pitch
150–200 words  ·  Written for a local journalist who does not know you
Structure it like this: Opening hook (what makes this interesting?) → What the shop is and what makes it different → Your story in one sentence → Opening date and location → A quote from you → Your name and contact details. Journalists are busy. Give them everything they need in 200 words.
Opening hook — what is the most interesting or specific thing about your shop?
One sentence on what makes you different from other options nearby
Your opening date and address
A quote from you — something specific, not generic
Example (for reference only — write your own)

"A new specialty coffee shop focused on single-origin filter coffee is opening in [neighbourhood] on [date]. [Shop name], owned by [name], will offer rotating seasonal coffees sourced directly from small producers, alongside a short menu of house-made pastries. Unlike the chain options nearby, [shop name] is designed as a place to slow down — with communal tables, no wi-fi passwords, and staff who can tell you exactly where your coffee came from. 'I wanted to open the kind of place I always wished existed here,' says [name]. 'Somewhere that takes the coffee seriously without taking itself too seriously.' [Shop name] opens at [address] on [date]. For press enquiries: [email]."

Format B
The Instagram Caption
50–80 words  ·  Written as an actual post, not a description of one
Rules: Write the caption, not a plan for it. No brand language. No "we are thrilled to announce." Read it out loud — if it sounds like a press release, rewrite it until it sounds like you talking. End with something people can do: follow, share, tell a friend, come find you.
First line — the one thing that makes someone stop scrolling
The human bit — why you, why here, in your own voice
The call to action — what do you want them to do?
Example (for reference only)

"We've been building something here for a while now. [Shop name] opens in [neighbourhood] on [date] — a small specialty café where the coffee is the point and the room feels like it belongs to the street. Come find us. Tell someone who'd love it. We'll have the kettle on. 📍[address]"

Format C
The Neighbour Conversation
2–3 sentences  ·  What you say when someone on the street asks what you are opening
The test: Would you actually say this out loud, right now, to someone you just met? If you would not say it, rewrite it. This is not a tagline — it is a real answer a real person gives in a real conversation. The version that sounds most like you is usually the best one.
Example (for reference only)

"We're opening a coffee shop just around the corner on [street] — small place, really focused on the coffee itself. Filter, espresso, a few things to eat. We open on [date]. You should come in."
