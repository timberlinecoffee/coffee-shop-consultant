Section 2
Your Opening Story

Start by answering these three questions in a sentence or two each. Your answers are the raw material for all three story formats below.

The Core Story — Answer These First
Why are you opening a coffee shop?
Why here — this location, this neighbourhood?
Why now — what has made this the right moment?
Format A
The Press Pitch — 150–200 words, written for a local journalist
Lead with what is interesting or different, not just "new café opens." Include your opening date, your location, and a specific angle. End with your name and contact details.
Format B
The Instagram Caption — 50–80 words, written as an actual post
Write the caption itself, not a description of it. End with a call to action. Read it out loud — it should sound like you, not a brand.
Format C
The Neighbour Conversation — 2–3 sentences maximum
What you say when someone asks what you are opening. No jargon. Should sound exactly like how you actually talk.

Check: Does Format C sound like you talking? If you would not say it out loud at a bus stop, rewrite it. That version — the natural one — is almost always better than the polished one.

Your 30-Day Pre-Opening Marketing Plan

Map out the four weeks before opening. Do not try to do everything — choose the actions that make most sense for your concept, location, and capacity. Use the 30-Day Marketing Calendar for the full day-by-day version.

Week 2
Days 8–14

Week 3
Days 15–21
Build Anticipation — Are you running a soft opening? What comes before the doors open?
Week 4
Days 22–30
Open Strong — What does opening day look like?
Are you running a soft opening? (Yes / No / Maybe)
If yes — who will you invite and when?
Will you launch a loyalty program from day one?
If yes — what format (stamp card, app, other)?
What is the one thing you most want to communicate before you open?
Not your full story — just the single most important thing you want people to know or feel about your shop before they walk in.
