Coffee Shop Basecamp — 8-Week Cohort

Drink Recipe Cards

One card per drink on your menu. Print, laminate, and keep them behind the bar. Every barista should be able to pick up any card and make that drink correctly without asking anyone.

Week 4 — Menu Design, Coffee Program & Sourcing

How to use these: This file contains example cards for common café drinks, plus blank templates at the end. Fill in or edit the blank cards for every drink on your menu. Print at full size — two cards per page — then laminate and post them at the espresso bar, the filter station, and the cold drinks area. Update the version number and date any time the recipe changes so staff always know which version is current.

Espresso — Milk
Flat White
Medium cup · 160–180ml Dairy default
Dose
18g
Yield
36g
Time
27–32s
Milk Temp
60–65°C
Build Order
1
Rinse and warm the cup with hot water. Discard.
2
Grind and dose 18g. Distribute and tamp level.
3
Extract double shot into warm cup (27–32 seconds).
4
Steam 120–140ml dairy milk to 60–65°C. Texture: silky microfoam — no big bubbles.
5
Pour milk slowly from low, bringing foam through last. Aim for a defined white dot on top.
Quality Check
Rich, glossy surface. Coffee-forward — milk enhances, not dominates. Velvety mouthfeel. No harsh bitterness or sour notes. Served at drinking temperature.

3
Extract double shot into warm cup (27–32 seconds).

Steam 200–220ml dairy milk to 60–65°C. Silky microfoam, slightly more volume than a flat white.

Espresso — Milk
Cappuccino
Medium cup · 150–160ml Equal thirds
Dose
18g
Yield
36g
Time
27–32s
Milk Temp
60–65°C
Build Order
1
Rinse and warm the cup. Discard water.
2
Grind and dose 18g. Distribute and tamp level.
3
Extract double shot into warm cup (27–32 seconds).
4
Steam 100–120ml milk to 60–65°C. More foam than a latte — thick and dense, not dry.
5
Pour milk and foam together. The drink should be roughly ⅓ espresso, ⅓ milk, ⅓ foam.
Quality Check
Foam should hold its shape and not collapse into the cup. Bolder coffee flavour than a latte. Dense, creamy foam — not dry or airy. No visible separation at the bottom.

Filter Coffee
Batch Brew
Medium or large cup Fresh within 60 min
Ratio
1:16
Grind
Coarse
Water
92–96°C
Max Age
60 min
Brew Protocol
1
Weigh coffee to ratio (e.g. 60g coffee per 960ml water for a standard batch).
2
Use fresh filter paper. Rinse with hot water before brewing, discard rinse water.
3
Brew and record time on the batch log. Label the server with brew time.
4
Discard and brew fresh if older than 60 minutes. Never top up an old batch.
5
Serve in warmed cup. Offer milk on the side.
Quality Check
Clean, bright, flavourful. No bitter or sour finish. Clear in the cup — not cloudy or muddy. Tastes like the coffee, not like hot water. Discard if it tastes flat or stale.

Page 3 of 4 — Cold Drinks & Signature
v1.0
Cold — Espresso
Iced Latte
Large cup · over ice Dairy default
Dose
18g
Yield
36g
Time
27–32s
Milk
180ml cold
Build Order
1
Fill large cup ¾ full with ice. Use fresh ice, never old or cloudy ice.
2
Add 180ml cold milk directly over ice.
3
Extract double shot. Pour espresso directly over milk and ice.
4
Stir gently once — do not over-mix. Add lid and straw.
Quality Check
Clean separation visible initially — espresso on top of milk on ice. Fully integrated after one stir. Cold, refreshing, balanced. Cup should feel cold and condensation-free inside 30 seconds of service.
