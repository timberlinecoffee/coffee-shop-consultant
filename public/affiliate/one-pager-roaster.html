A Planning Resource for Your Coffee Shop Clients

From [Roaster Name]

Opening a coffee shop is one of the most demanding businesses your clients will ever take on. They've done the training. They know the craft. What they often don't have is a clear, structured plan for the business side.

Groundwork is a planning platform built specifically for specialty coffee shop operators.

It walks aspiring owners through the decisions that matter most before they sign a lease, place an equipment order, or hire their first team member — the planning phase where the difference between a sustainable shop and an expensive mistake is made.

What Groundwork covers

  • Financial modeling Build a realistic P&L, break-even analysis, and cash-flow projection before committing capital. No spreadsheet expertise required.
  • Concept and menu planning Price your drinks for profitability, not just for what "feels right." Understand your margins before you finalize your menu.
  • Buildout and equipment planning Know what you actually need for your space and service model before you walk into a supplier or sign a lease.
  • Operations checklists The pre-opening tasks most new operators miss until it's too late: permits, health inspections, supplier accounts, staffing timelines.
  • Guided milestones A structured roadmap from "I want to open a shop" to "I'm ready to open a shop," broken into phases your clients can actually follow.
  • Ongoing co-pilot Not a course. A working tool for the real decisions that come after the coffee education is done.

Who it's for

Groundwork is built for clients who are 3–18 months from opening — aspiring specialty coffee operators who've completed their training and are now doing the serious planning work. It's a co-pilot for the business decisions that come after the coffee education.
