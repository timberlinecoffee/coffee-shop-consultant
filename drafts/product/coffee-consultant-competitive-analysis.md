# Competitive Analysis: Coffee Shop Planning & Coaching Tools

**Prepared by:** Chief of Staff  
**Date:** April 17, 2026  
**For:** Timberline Coffee School — My Coffee Shop Consultant Platform  
**Task:** [TIM-100](/TIM/issues/TIM-100)

---

## Executive Summary

There is **no direct competitor** to what we're building. The market has two distinct clusters — operational POS/management software (irrelevant to planning-stage users) and generic business plan generators — with a third cluster of human consultants and course-based programs. No existing product combines coffee-specific expertise, AI coaching, and a structured planning-to-BRD workflow in a self-serve SaaS format. This is the gap. Marcus can't find what we're building — because it doesn't exist yet.

The closest threat is not a competitor: it's inertia. Marcus googles "how to open a coffee shop," finds a free Homebase blog post and a Crimson Cup book for $69, and decides he knows enough. Our job is to be the thing he finds instead — and the thing that's obviously more valuable than any of those alternatives combined.

---

## Landscape Map

There are four categories to assess:

1. Coffee-specific planning / consulting programs
2. Generic business planning SaaS
3. Coffee management software (POS/ops tools)
4. Online courses and communities

## Category 1: Coffee-Specific Planning & Consulting Programs

### Crimson Cup — 7 Steps to Success
**URL:** crimsoncup.com/open  
**Pricing:** $69.99 entry (book + proforma template); full consulting program has no published price (custom engagement, implicitly $5,000+)  
**Key features:**
- 300+ shops opened across 37 states using this methodology
- Book-based framework with an interactive proforma tool for cost estimation
- Full consulting package includes pre-opening support, 5 days on-site training, equipment package
- Coffee bar design services, supplier relationships built in

**Strengths:** Proven track record; real hands-on support; bundled equipment sourcing creates genuine value; comprehensive from planning through opening

**Weaknesses:**
- The $69.99 entry tier is a book. It is not software. It doesn't adapt to the user.
- The full program requires buying coffee from Crimson Cup — they're a roaster, so there's a supply relationship embedded. Marcus may not want to commit to a specific roaster before he's even opened.
- Not self-serve; full program requires human engagement (consulting calls, on-site visits)
- US-centric; no Calgary/Canada-specific content

**Marcus Test:** Marcus would buy the $69.99 book because it feels safe and affordable. But a book doesn't answer his specific questions, doesn't update based on his inputs, and doesn't produce a real BRD. He'd consume it, feel semi-informed, and still be stuck on "okay but what's my first actual step." We beat this because our platform builds WITH him, not at him.

### Bellissimo Coffee Advisors (coffeebusiness.com)
**URL:** coffeebusiness.com  
**Pricing:** Consulting from $250–$1,125; startup bundle (course + 2 eBooks + 2 hrs 1:1) at a fixed price (not publicly stated — requires contact)  
**Key features:**
- 25+ years in coffee consulting
- Craver Coffee Business Startup Bundle: course + How To Open a Coffee Shop eBook + 1:1 consultation
- Equipment sourcing assistance with vendor discounts
- Online course + consulting hybrid

**Strengths:** Deep expertise; genuinely personalized human consultation; vendor discount relationships

**Weaknesses:**
- Gated behind human consultation — not self-serve
- Course content appears static (no AI adaptation)
- Pricing for the full bundle not transparent; requires initiating contact (friction for Marcus)
- No structured BRD output or living document

**Marcus Test:** Marcus would never email a consultant. He wants to start right now, tonight, after his shift. Consultation-gated products don't match his workflow. We win by being available at 11 PM when he's excited.

### Coffee Shop Startups (coffeeshopstartups.com)
**URL:** coffeeshopstartups.com  
**Pricing:** Not publicly listed on main site; hosted on LearnWorlds (platform cost $29–$299/mo for the vendor)  
**Key features:**
- Blog and free guides (strong SEO presence)
- Startup kits and courses — "helped thousands worldwide since 2013"
- Community aspect; covers concept through launch

**Strengths:** Strong organic reach; accessible brand; years of content; real community signal

**Weaknesses:**
- Primary product appears to be content/courses, not software
- No AI coaching; no living document system; no financial modeling tool
- Static templates/checklists, not an adaptive planning system
- No visibility into pricing — poor conversion optimization

**Marcus Test:** Marcus would find this in Google and read 4 blog posts for free. He'd never pay for the course because the free content is good enough and he can't see what he'd get for his money. We need to be the thing he finds AFTER he's realized free blog posts aren't enough to actually build a plan.

### Texas Coffee School
**URL:** texascoffeeschool.com  
**Pricing:** 3-Day Coffee Business Master Class (pricing not publicly listed — contact required)  
**Key features:**
- In-person intensive program
- Covers business planning, equipment, operations
- Barista skills training component

**Strengths:** Hands-on, high-quality education; real skills training alongside business planning

**Weaknesses:**

- No AI, no living document, no BRD output

**Marcus Test:** Not relevant to Marcus — he can't afford to fly to Texas and take a week off work. We don't compete here; we complement (our platform → Basecamp → hands-on training is a better local stack for Marcus).

## Category 2: Generic Business Planning SaaS

### LivePlan
**URL:** liveplan.com  
**Pricing:** Standard $15/mo (annual) / $20/mo (monthly); Premium $30/mo (annual) / $40/mo (monthly)  
**Key features:**
- Step-by-step business plan builder
- Financial forecast templates (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow)
- Pitch decks and investor-ready outputs
- 500+ sample business plans including food & beverage

**Strengths:** Well-established (#1 business plan software brand); strong financial modeling; integrates with QuickBooks; sample plans for coffee shops exist

**Weaknesses:**
- Completely generic — no coffee-specific knowledge, terminology, or coaching
- No AI coaching; no adaptive guidance based on user inputs
- Financial templates require the user to already know their numbers; doesn't help estimate startup costs or validate assumptions
- No module structure guiding them through what they need to decide and in what order
- Produces a "business plan" suitable for banks — but doesn't help Marcus figure out WHAT his business actually is first

**Marcus Test:** Marcus would sign up for LivePlan, open the financial forecast section, see a spreadsheet asking for "Cost of Goods Sold" and "Operating Expenses," and close the browser. Our platform wins by being coffee-first — Marcus doesn't have to be a finance person to start.

---

### Upmetrics
**URL:** upmetrics.co  
**Pricing:** Premium $14–$19/mo (annual); Professional $37–$49/mo (annual); starting from $7/mo  
**Key features:**
- AI writing assistant (auto-writes business plan sections)
- Financial forecasting up to 10 years
- 400+ sample plans; 400+ templates
- Pitch deck generator; QuickBooks/Xero integration
- Investor-ready outputs

**Strengths:** Modern AI-powered tool; genuinely useful financial forecasting; lower price point than LivePlan; actively maintained

**Weaknesses:**
- Same fundamental problem as LivePlan: it's a document generator, not a thinking partner
- The AI writes sections based on prompts, but doesn't KNOW anything about running a coffee shop
- No domain-specific knowledge: won't tell Marcus that espresso machine prices range from $8K to $25K depending on volume, won't flag that most new owners underestimate first-year revenue
- The output is a standard business plan document — not a coffee-specific BRD with module-level exercises and deliverables

**Marcus Test:** Marcus would find this affordable and initially appealing. He'd use the AI to write an executive summary. It would produce competent but generic prose. He'd feel like he's making progress but actually just have a document, not a real plan. When his bank asks hard questions about his concept, he won't have the underlying thinking to answer them. We win because we build the thinking, not just the document.

## Category 3: Coffee Management Software (POS & Operations)

**Toast POS, Square for Restaurants, Lightspeed Restaurant, joe Coffee** — these are NOT competitors. They serve existing shop owners managing daily operations. Marcus has nothing to manage yet. These tools are irrelevant to planning-stage users.

When Marcus does open his shop, these are the tools he'll use. **Opportunity note:** this creates a natural referral/integration partnership play. We could become the onboarding ramp that directs users toward specific POS platforms at Module 5 or 8, earning referral commissions. (Flag for separate analysis — see TIM-101.)

## Category 4: Online Courses and Communities

### Udemy — "How to Start a Coffee Shop"
**Pricing:** $15–$25 (frequent sales)  
**Strengths:** Cheap, immediately accessible, credible platform  
**Weaknesses:** Static video content; no AI; no personalized output; no ongoing support; Marcus watches it on 1.5x speed and feels like he's done something

**Marcus Test:** Marcus might buy this before he finds us. This is a top-of-funnel competitor, not mid-funnel. Our landing page should speak directly to the person who's already watched YouTube and taken a Udemy course but still doesn't have a real plan.

---

### SCA Training (Specialty Coffee Association)
**URL:** sca.training  
**Pricing:** Course-by-course; certification programs range from $100–$1,000+  
**Strengths:** Industry-recognized credentials; rigorous technical coffee education  
**Weaknesses:** Barista/roasting skills focus, not business planning; no SaaS; expensive credentials

## The Gap: What Nobody Builds

After surveying the full landscape, the market gap is clear and specific:

**There is no product that:**
1. Is built specifically for the coffee shop planning stage (pre-opening, pre-capital)
2. Provides adaptive AI coaching that knows coffee — not generic business writing
3. Guides users through a structured, opinionated decision sequence (concept → financials → site → menu → bar → team → marketing → BRD)
4. Produces a living, interconnected Business Readiness Document that updates as decisions change
5. Is self-serve, accessible at 11 PM, and priced for someone who hasn't raised capital yet

Generic business plan software is too finance-heavy and too generic. Coffee consulting programs are too human-gated and expensive. Online courses are static. POS software is for operators, not planners.

**Our platform sits in an unoccupied position.**

## Competitive Positioning Recommendation

Position "My Coffee Shop Consultant" as: **"The first intelligent planning platform built for people who want to open a coffee shop — not a blank business plan template, not a $200/hr consultant, not a Udemy course you'll never finish."**

The three comparison points to own in marketing:

| What they'd otherwise do | Problem | Why we win |
|---|---|---|
| Buy Crimson Cup's book / take a Udemy course | Static content doesn't adapt; no real output | AI coaches adapt to THEIR shop concept; produces real BRD |
| Use LivePlan / Upmetrics | Generic — doesn't know coffee; finance-heavy UX | Coffee-first — built for Marcus, not MBA students |
| Hire a consultant | $200/hr; not available at 11 PM; intimidating | $49/mo; available anytime; feels like a knowledgeable friend |

## The Marcus Test — Final Assessment

Would Marcus find and use any of these competitors instead of our platform?

- **Crimson Cup book ($69.99):** Yes, possibly — if he's already committed and wants a tangible artifact. Risk: he buys the book and feels like he's done his research. **Mitigation:** Our landing page should speak to book buyers who are still stuck.
- **Upmetrics ($7–$19/mo):** Yes, possibly — it's cheap and feels modern. Risk: he signs up, generates a generic doc, and thinks he's ready. **Mitigation:** We need to be findable on the same searches (coffee shop business plan software) and win on specificity.
- **Free blog posts (Homebase, Toast, etc.):** Very likely his first step. These are top-of-funnel leads for us, not competitors. **Action:** SEO strategy should target users who've consumed this content and are ready for the next step.
- **Our platform:** He would choose us when he realizes that having read 10 articles and a book, he still doesn't have an actual plan. We are the "okay, now I'm serious" product.

**The platform's pricing ($49 Builder) is above the impulse-purchase threshold but well below consulting costs. This is correct positioning.** Justification: see TIM-101 for pricing validation analysis.
